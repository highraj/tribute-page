Dr. Manmohan Singh

Renowned Economist, Statesman, and the 13th Prime Minister of India (2004-2014)

About Dr. Manmohan Singh

Dr. Manmohan Singh is an Indian economist, academic, and politician who served as the Prime Minister of India for two consecutive terms from 2004 to 2014. Known for his humble demeanor and exceptional economic reforms, he played a pivotal role in shaping modern India's economy. As the architect of India's 1991 economic liberalization, he is widely respected as a visionary leader.

Achievements

  • Architect of India's Economic Reforms (1991)
  • Served as Governor of the Reserve Bank of India (1982-1985)
  • Awarded Padma Vibhushan in 1987
  • Served as India's Finance Minister (1991-1996)
  • Led India as the Prime Minister for a decade (2004-2014)
